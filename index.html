Results

Given any \(n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\) and \(k \in [0,2n]\), we have \(|\{S : (S \subseteq [0,n]) \land (k \not \in 2S)\}| = 2^{|n-k|} \cdot 3^{\lfloor (n-|n-k|+1)/2 \rfloor}\).   Oct 16 2021

Notation

I use \([a,b]\) to denote the integer interval \(\{a,a+1,...,b\}\).
I use \(2A\) to denote the sumset \(A+A = \{a_1+a_2 : a_1,a_2 \in A\}\).
